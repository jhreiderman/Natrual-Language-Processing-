Utilizing various natural language processing methodologies, this project sought to analyze and uncover properties associated with public discourse on various subreddits relative to the Israel – Hamas war. Public discourse data taken off Reddit was utilized to generate various models associated with the following methodologies: Latent Dirichlet Analysis, Vader Sentiment Analysis, Principial Component Analysis (PCA). The results showed that the most significant topics of discussion related to the conflict were the following: Drawing attention to the decision making process of the Israeli government as it relates to the conflict in Palestine (with possible emphasis on enacting defiant economic behavior (boycott) as a way of protesting the ongoing conflict), allegations of genocide against the Israeli government, and Israel’s military conduct going after terrorists that might be embedded in civilian population areas (and collateral civilian damage caused by those military operations). The Vader sentiment analysis highlighted that a majority of the individuals captured in the data do not feel strongly, either positively or negatively, about the conflict. However, distinct differences are present when analyzing the frequency of negative to positive as related to particular sentiment scores, with a higher density of respondents feeling negatively about the conflict. Finally, the results of the PCA analysis showed that the most defining characteristics / features driving public discourse are Israel and Hamas. Taken together, this analysis presents an introductory assessment of the semantic space and emotional properties associated with the public discourse that was captured on Reddit.
